Learnings

All companies are performing a balancing act.

What's pulling on one end is the drive for growth and the natural timing of investment to return of capital.

On the other, a financial stack that's limited.

Understanding the financial stack of Clearco and how it directly related to business activities, is the most critical business lesson I've learned so far.

I've always wanted a rounded skill-set - but it is in this role that I've started to understand how important it is to see different sides of a company.

Although it (really) was exciting to rebuild some of our companies financial reporting - the role is too internal.

I want to build more for people, to be at the intersection of the internal and external.

The reasons I left are:

  • Wanted to work on something that supports artists (overall vision).
  • Wanted to work more at the intersection of internal and external.
  • Wanted to have a different work culture.

I'm now looking to join a larger team where I can leverage my diverse skill set to provide meaningful impact.

Learnings:

  • Diversify your financial stack to align with purpose of capital.
  • Data cleanliness is critical to control.
  • Know executive priorities to provide relevant reporting.
  • Although building quickly is important, it's important to set time apart to solve for tech debt.
  • To successfully execute a project as a team, it's critical to align priorities and remove blockers.
