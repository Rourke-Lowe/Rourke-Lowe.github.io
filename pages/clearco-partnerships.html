Clearco - Partnerships


Situation

Vision: Help founders win..

Mission: Eliminate barriers to access of capital.

Returning to Toronto, I was excited about two companies in particular:

  • Ada (then Ada Support)
  • Clearco (then Clearbanc)

Both of the positions were exciting -- at Ada I would join as a technical sales consultant for integrating their AI customer success automation tool, at Clearco I would be leading up new partnerships initiatives.

The criteria I evaluated against were:

  • How much will I learn about Sales.
  • How much will I learn about Finance.
  • How much will I be able to manage myself.
  • Which culture seemed 'faster'.

Clearco was giving me everything I wanted.

The opportunity to understand how capital flows, the ability to lead up a new initiative and manage all metrics, and the opportunity to practice my sales ability.

Beyond this, Clearco's vision was to empower all businesses with easier access to capital for growth. With Peersight ultimately failing due to a lack of capital - I was motivated.

After being home for 2 weeks, I joined Clearco as the first Partnerships Services Manager.

Projects

Build a network of consultants who refer business and help grow our portfolio.

  • Develop pitch & pitch deck targeted towards consultants in eCommerce and SaaS.
  • Targeted high traffic, low ranking difficulty companies to generate high volume of users through organic search.
  • Build pipeline of 1000 self prospected leads, messaging an average of 50 per day with 5-8 video calls.
  • Developed 6 strong partnerships, referring ~$2,000 in MRR per month.

Goal: Develop a community of accountants who refer companies in need of capital, and help our portfolio manage their finances.

  • Develop pitch & pitch deck targeted towards eCommerce and SaaS accountants.
  • Built a pipeline of 100 high value accountants, developed and executed named account strategy for each account.
  • Developed 15 strong partnerships, referring ~$3,000 MRR per month.
  • Facilitated introductions of ~10 businesses per month to the accounting partner that best fit their needs.

Goal: Rebuild our accounting export to better service our funded businesses and accounting partners.

  • Accounting information on our funding was very difficult to understand and export, making partnerships with accountants difficult to maintain, and resulted in churn from financially savvy customers.
  • Drafted minimum viable product through product discovery interviews with accountants and funded businesses.
  • Received executive alignment on need for improved reporting.
  • Worked with Product and Engineering to implement and iterate on product.

Learnings

The unique position of building out a net new area of partnerships gave me the opportunity to work both inside the business (creating new content & strategy for partnerships etc.), and outside the business (developing the partnerships).

Sales was truly the backbone of Clearco. Without a team constantly focussed on matching our product with other businesses needs, we would have had a minute percentage of the revenue we did.

Managing the initiative became a fun exercise. My manager would have only an hour a week to help solve my problems. Each week we'd meet to share performance metrics, and unblock potential upcoming problems.

Through these short interactions, I was able to improve my ability to foresee what might be potential blockers for achieving my OKRs, and clearly report on my performance.

With the start of COVID, the team decided to restructure the organization in fear of the impact of a rapidly declining economy, including removing what was the Partnerships team.

Learnings:

  • Set objectives with measurable key results - review alignment weekly.
  • To gain interest in a product, you must understand the buyers needs.
  • Managing expectations clearly builds trust and eliminates mis-understanding.
  • Increasing close rate is only useful if the activity metrics are there.
  • Anyone could be a potential client or partner.
